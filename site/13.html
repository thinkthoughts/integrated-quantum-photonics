Two-Mode Squeezing

Four-wave mixing produces correlated frequency pairs whose quantum fluctuations become the fundamental resource for scalable continuous-variable photonic quantum computing.

Navigation: Index · 00 · 01 · 05 · 07 · 11 · 13 · 17 · 23 · 29 · 31 · 37 · 43 · 49 · 53 · 61 · 67

Overview

Kerr nonlinearity generates more than additional optical frequencies. Energy conservation creates correlated signal and idler modes whose joint quantum state exhibits squeezing and entanglement.

Rather than treating every frequency independently, pairs become the elementary quantum resource from which larger photonic quantum states are assembled. 


Kerr interaction
→ four-wave mixing
→ signal–idler pair
→ two-mode squeezing
→ correlated quantum resource
→ scalable entanglement

Notebook 13 explains how nonlinear optics becomes quantum information through correlated optical mode pairs.
