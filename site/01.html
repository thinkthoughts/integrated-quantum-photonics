Notebook 01

Scaling Problem

Adding more quantum devices eventually scales packaging, alignment, routing, yield, calibration, and measurement faster than useful quantum resources. This notebook motivates frequency multiplexing as the next scalable direction.

Navigation: Index · 00 · 01 · 05 · 07 · 11 · 13 · 17 · 23 · 29 · 31 · 37 · 43 · 49 · 61 · 53 · 67

Overview

Early photonic quantum experiments often scale by adding another source, detector, fiber, package, or control channel. Initially this is effective, but every new component increases alignment effort, fabrication variability, calibration overhead, routing complexity, and optical loss.

The engineering question therefore becomes: 


Can one physical device
produce many useful quantum modes?

instead of

Can we build many identical devices?

Notebook 01 identifies replication as the dominant systems constraint and motivates modal scaling through integrated frequency combs.
