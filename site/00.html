Why Integrated Quantum Photonics?

The anchor notebook for the repository: why quantum photonics shifts from adding more devices to exploiting many optical modes on integrated chips.

Navigation: Index · 00 · 01 · 05 · 07 · 11 · 13 · 17 · 23 · 29 · 31 · 37 · 43 · 49 · 53 · 61 · 67

Overview

Integrated quantum photonics starts from a scaling pressure: quantum computing, communication, and sensing need many usable optical quantum resources, but simple device replication creates packaging, yield, routing, and measurement constraints.

Notebook 00 reframes the problem as a transition from hardware replication toward modal resource management: frequency multiplexing, quantum frequency combs, Kerr nonlinear interaction, squeezing, many-mode entanglement, integrated circuits, and loss characterization.

Key equations

Resonator modes:

\[ \omega_m=\omega_0+mD_1. \]

Free spectral range:

\[ D_1=2\pi\times\mathrm{FSR}. \]

Quality factor:

\[ Q=\frac{\omega_0}{\kappa}. \]

Scaling objective:

\[ N_{\rm modes}\gg N_{\rm devices}. \]

Engineering principle:

\[ \text{Modal scaling}>\text{device replication}. \]
