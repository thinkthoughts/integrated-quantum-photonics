Notebook 29

Loss as Design Constraint

Loss determines which photonic resources remain usable. This notebook treats loss as an engineering constraint that reshapes the ideal graph into an admissible computational resource.

Navigation: Index · 00 · 01 · 05 · 07 · 11 · 13 · 17 · 23 · 29 · 31 · 37 · 43 · 49 · 53 · 61 · 67

Overview

Cluster-state photonic architectures need large entangled graphs, but every realistic implementation introduces loss through resonators, couplers, routing, filters, detectors, packaging, and measurement.

Notebook 29 treats loss as a constraint on admissible computation: the question is not only whether an ideal graph can be written down, but which parts of the resource survive the full optical budget.
