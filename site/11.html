Kerr Nonlinearity

Kerr nonlinearity provides the physical mechanism that couples frequency modes through four-wave mixing, enabling quantum frequency comb generation inside integrated resonators.

Navigation: Index · 00 · 01 · 05 · 07 · 11 · 13 · 17 · 23 · 29 · 31 · 37 · 43 · 49 · 53 · 61 · 67

Overview

Frequency combs require more than evenly spaced resonator modes. A nonlinear interaction must exchange energy between optical frequencies while conserving energy and momentum.

Integrated microresonators accomplish this through the Kerr nonlinear optical response. Pump photons generate correlated sidebands through four-wave mixing, allowing the frequency comb to expand across many resonant modes.
