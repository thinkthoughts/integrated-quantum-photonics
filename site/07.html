Notebook 07

Quantum Frequency Combs

Frequency combs turn one integrated resonator into a structured family of optical modes. The resource shifts from replicated devices to addressable, evenly spaced frequency channels.

Navigation: Index · 00 · 01 · 05 · 07 · 11 · 13 · 17 · 23 · 29 · 31 · 37 · 43 · 49 · 53 · 61 · 67

Overview

Notebook 07 explains how integrated resonators support discrete optical modes that can be used as a frequency-domain resource. Instead of building one device per quantum channel, a single resonator can support many addressable modes.

The key engineering transition is: 

ring resonator
→ discrete modes
→ frequency spacing
→ quantum frequency comb
→ many usable optical channels

This prepares the next step: understanding how Kerr nonlinearity couples modes and generates squeezing.
